How to read

Something that people often seem to have trouble doing is reading an article and actually learning something from it. Here are a few tips for doing that:

  • Understand: You will probably get more out of an article if you understand rather than memorise what is being said.
  • Think: If you see something that insprires you or provokes thought, take the time to deviaite from reading the text and work things out on your own.
  • Simplify: Break things down into simple parts to understand them, then put them back together to understand the whole concept.

I probably could have written an entire article on this, but really it's simple. Actually, most things are that way: simple, until some textbook author comes to obfuscate ideas with brick walls of text. If you really need more, search the Feynman Technique.

TL;DR: Reading large articles and textbooks is just a matter of reverse engineering to find out the obvious.
